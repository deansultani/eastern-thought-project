Where to Begin

The Oldest Questions,
Still Fresh

Eastern philosophy is not a single stream but a vast delta — dozens of traditions, centuries deep, flowing from India, China, Tibet, Japan, and beyond. What they share is a willingness to sit with the fundamental questions: What is the nature of mind? How should we live? What lies beneath the surface of things?

This project exists to offer a clear path into that territory. We start with one of the most translated texts in human history: the Tao Te Ching. Eighty-one short chapters. Barely five thousand words. Enough to change how you see everything.

From there, we open outward — to Vedanta and Hindu philosophy, to Tibetan Buddhist teachings, to Zen, to the Upanishads. Each tradition illuminates the others, and all of them illuminate us.

"The Tao that can be told is not the eternal Tao. The name that can be named is not the eternal name."

Tao Te Ching, Chapter 1 — tr. Stephen Mitchell

Mitchell's translation is the one we return to again and again. Where other versions can feel obscure or archaic, his reads with the clarity of a window just cleaned — you see right through the words to the original light. He brings decades of spiritual study to every line, and the result is a book you can open to any page and find something worth sitting with.

Whether you are brand new to Eastern thought or returning after years of practice, this is the edition to keep on your shelf.

The Landscape

Traditions We Explore

道

Taoism

The way of water and emptiness. Rooted in the writings of Laozi and Zhuangzi, Taoism invites a return to the natural flow beneath all effort.

☸

Tibetan Buddhism

Rich with ritual, symbol, and practice — Tibetan Buddhism offers a complete map of the mind and a path through the bardos of experience.

ॐ

Vedanta & Hinduism

From the Upanishads to the Bhagavad Gita, the Hindu traditions ask the deepest question: What is Atman? What is Brahman? Are they one?

🪷

Zen Buddhism

Don't think — sit. Zen strips away doctrine and meets us in the present moment, through koans, breath, and the simple act of showing up.

🧘

Meditation

Across every Eastern tradition, meditation is the laboratory. Sitting still, you begin to see clearly what is always already here.

📜

Sacred Texts

The Tao Te Ching, the Upanishads, the Dhammapada, the Gita — these texts have survived millennia because they speak to something permanent in us.

"When you realize there is nothing lacking, the whole world belongs to you."
Tao Te Ching, Chapter 44 — tr. Stephen Mitchell
